Show me some galaxies!

These are 3''x3'' images of one of our objects in different NIRCam and MIRI filters. Left-most panel shows the image in NIRCam/F277W filter whose 80% enclosed point source energy radius (0.179'') is used as the aperture size to measure the central flux which forms the red points in the Figure 2 of the poster. This aperture was fixed for all filters and is shown by red circle in each image. The black dotted line in the left-most panel gives the Kron radius for this source used by CANDELS to measure the source flux reported in their catalogues that forms our the black points in the Figure 2 of the poster. The black star shows the co-ordinates of the optical counterpart given by AEGIS-X. The corrected centres in IR are shown by red star in the left-most panel.
